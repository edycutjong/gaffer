The problem

Commentary dies exactly
when the fan needs it most.

  • 01Expert commentary needs a cloud connection.Away end, flaky stadium wifi, a plane — the app just spins, right when the fan needs the explainer.
  • 02The fans who need it most hold the weakest phones.A three-year-old handset can't run a capable model fast enough to feel live — on-device alone tops out around 6 tok/s.

"I was in the away end with no signal and a three-year-old phone — the cloud commentary app spun forever and I missed why the ref pointed to the spot."

→ so the fix can't be a bigger cloud. It has to be no cloud at all.

The solution

A pundit that runs on your device — and borrows a peer's when it can't.

  • ◆On-device by default.QVAC's completion({stream:true}) narrates every press, overlap and penalty token-by-token from a football system prompt — entirely on your phone.
  • ◆Borrows a peer's compute when it can't keep up.A laptop in the room announces itself as a provider on the match's Hyperswarm topic; your phone's completion requests stream over a Noise-encrypted socket — peers are keypairs, not IP addresses.
  • ◆Never drops the sentence.A fully-tested state machine — LOCAL ⇄ OFFLOADED ⇄ FALLBACK — resumes on-device the instant a peer disappears, token-exact with the deterministic engine.

→ the offload is the product. Everything else got cut.

Architecture · why only QVAC × Pear

One substrate. Not a bolt-on.

QVACThe mind, on-device. loadModel({modelSrc:'pear://…'}) · completion({stream:true}) → tokenStream · textToSpeech() (Piper) · startQVACProvider({topic}) when available.
PearThe transport, peer-to-peer. Hyperswarm match rooms · Noise Secretstream E2E · Hyperdrive model sharing over pear:// · HyperDHT holepunching.
CoreThe honesty layer. lib/state.js — every (state × event) pair defined and tested; lib/router.js — strict token ordering, token-exact resume on deterministic engines.

Take QVAC + Pear out and you'd need a cloud LLM, a cloud TTS, a model CDN, a signalling/TURN server and a rented GPU — plus a privacy policy asking fans to trust you.
